# ROAM Product Overview

## Product Name
ROAM

## One-Sentence Summary
ROAM is a local freight marketplace web app where people who need dump trucks or flatbeds can post loads, nearby drivers get notified, drivers submit bids, and the load poster accepts one bid to assign the job.

## Beta Goal
Build a testable beta that proves the core marketplace loop works from end to end:
1. A shipper posts a load.
2. Relevant drivers can see the load.
3. Drivers submit bids.
4. The shipper accepts or declines bids.
5. One driver wins and the load becomes assigned.

## Primary Users
- Shipper / Poster: person or company posting a load that needs a dump truck or flatbed.
- Driver / Carrier: person with an account who can view nearby loads and place a bid.
- Admin: internal role to monitor users, loads, bids, and disputes.

## Problem to Solve
Phone calls, texts, and manual dispatching are slow and hard to track. ROAM should make load matching faster, easier, and more transparent.

## Core Value Proposition
- Faster matching between load posters and available drivers.
- Competitive bidding from local drivers.
- Clear assignment workflow.
- Trackable status from posted to assigned.

## Beta Principles
- Web app first, mobile responsive.
- Fast to test, not overbuilt.
- Simple workflows over edge-case perfection.
- Use clear status labels and role-based views.
- Prefer in-app notifications and email for beta; avoid overcomplicated dispatch logic in V1.

## Out of Scope for Beta
- Full payments and escrow.
- Ratings and reviews.
- Advanced compliance verification.
- Route optimization.
- Real-time GPS tracking.
- Multi-stop loads.
- Complex broker workflows.
- Native mobile apps.

## Success Criteria for Beta
- New user can sign up and log in.
- Shipper can create a load in under 3 minutes.
- Driver can discover an open load and place a bid.
- Shipper can compare bids and accept one.
- Winning driver sees the load as assigned.
- Other drivers see that the load is no longer available.
